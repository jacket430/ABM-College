The Stupid Blue Ring (Traveling Light)

Behold Calgary's most "beloved" public art installation - the magnificent Blue Ring! This stunning piece of "art" (we use the term loosely) cost taxpayers a mere $470,000 and has been the subject of much "appreciation" since its installation in 2013. It's not just a ring, it's a symbol of... well, we're still trying to figure that out.

Artistic Significance

This masterpiece offers profound insights into... something:

The Concept

According to the artist, this represents "a portal to the city" and "a lens through which to view Calgary." We're pretty sure it's just a big blue ring, but what do we know about art?

The Materials

Constructed from high-quality steel and painted in a shade of blue that perfectly matches the sky (when it's not cloudy, which is never), this piece truly represents the pinnacle of... something.

The Location

Strategically placed at the entrance to the city, this ring serves as a perfect welcome to visitors, who immediately understand that Calgary has... interesting taste in public art.

The Maintenance

Requires regular cleaning and occasional repainting, because nothing says "valuable public investment" like a giant blue ring that needs constant upkeep.

Visitor Experience

  • Take selfies that make it look like you're wearing a giant blue earring
  • Count how many people drive by without noticing it
  • Debate whether it's worth $470,000 (spoiler: it's not)
  • Wonder why it's called "Traveling Light" when it clearly isn't going anywhere
  • Try to spot the artistic value (good luck with that)

Visitor Information

Location

96th Avenue NE and Deerfoot Trail, Calgary, AB

Conveniently located where you can't miss it, no matter how hard you try.

Hours of Operation

  • Open 24/7 (because it's literally just a ring)
  • Best viewed while driving by at 100 km/h
  • No admission fee (you've already paid for it with your taxes)
  • No guided tours (thank goodness)

Accessibility

The Blue Ring is fully accessible, which is impressive considering it's just a ring:

  • Visible from the highway
  • No stairs required
  • No need to get out of your car
  • Perfect for drive-by viewing
  • Service animals welcome (though they probably won't care)

Plan Your Visit

To make the most of your visit to the Blue Ring, we recommend:

  • Setting your expectations very, very low
  • Bringing a camera to document this "artistic achievement"
  • Preparing your best "I don't get it" face
  • Calculating how many potholes could have been fixed for $470,000
  • Remembering that at least it's not as controversial as the Bowfort Towers

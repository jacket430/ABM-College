Fort Calgary

Discover the birthplace of Calgary at Fort Calgary, where the city's story began in 1875. This National Historic Site marks the confluence of the Bow and Elbow Rivers, where the North-West Mounted Police established their first post in the region.

Historical Significance

Fort Calgary played a crucial role in the development of Western Canada:

NWMP Origins

Learn about the North-West Mounted Police's establishment of the fort and their role in maintaining peace and order in the region.

Indigenous Relations

Explore the complex relationships between the NWMP and Indigenous peoples, including the Blackfoot Confederacy and the Métis.

Urban Development

Discover how the fort's presence led to the development of Calgary as a major urban center in Western Canada.

Archaeological Discoveries

See artifacts and learn about the archaeological work that has helped uncover the fort's history.

Visitor Experiences

  • Guided tours of the historic site
  • Interactive exhibits in the Interpretive Centre
  • Special events and reenactments
  • Educational programs for schools
  • Walking trails with interpretive signage

Location

750 9th Avenue SE, Calgary, AB T2G 5E1

Located at the confluence of the Bow and Elbow Rivers, easily accessible from downtown Calgary.

Plan Your Visit

To make the most of your visit to Fort Calgary, we recommend:

  • Allowing 1-2 hours to explore the site and exhibits
  • Checking the schedule for guided tours and special events
  • Bringing comfortable walking shoes for exploring the grounds
  • Visiting the gift shop for unique historical souvenirs
  • Exploring the nearby RiverWalk and other downtown attractions

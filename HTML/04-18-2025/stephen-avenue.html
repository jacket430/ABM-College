Stephen Avenue Walk

Experience the heart of Calgary's downtown at Stephen Avenue Walk, a National Historic District that blends historic architecture with modern urban life. This pedestrian-friendly street is home to some of Calgary's most iconic buildings and vibrant street culture.

Historic Architecture

Stephen Avenue showcases Calgary's architectural evolution through its historic buildings:

Bankers' Row

Explore the impressive bank buildings that once formed Calgary's financial district, featuring stunning Beaux-Arts and Classical Revival architecture.

Hudson's Bay Company

Visit the historic Hudson's Bay Company building, a landmark department store that has served Calgarians since 1913.

Calgary Tower

Discover the iconic Calgary Tower, a symbol of the city's growth and development since 1968.

Historic Storefronts

Admire the preserved storefronts and facades that tell the story of Calgary's commercial past.

Modern Attractions

  • Boutique shopping and local artisans
  • Diverse dining options from casual to fine dining
  • Street performers and public art installations
  • Seasonal festivals and events
  • Access to Calgary's cultural institutions

Visitor Information

Location

8th Avenue SW between 1st Street SW and 3rd Street SE

Located in the heart of downtown Calgary, easily accessible by public transit and the CTrain.

Best Times to Visit

  • Weekdays: 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM (shopping hours)
  • Evenings: Dining and entertainment
  • Weekends: Street festivals and events
  • Summer: Outdoor patios and street performers
  • Winter: Holiday decorations and events

Accessibility

Stephen Avenue is a pedestrian-only zone during the day, making it easily accessible for all visitors. The area is wheelchair accessible and includes:

  • Ramped curbs and smooth pavement
  • Accessible public washrooms
  • Nearby accessible parking
  • CTrain stations with elevator access

Plan Your Visit

To make the most of your visit to Stephen Avenue, we recommend:

  • Exploring during the day to appreciate the architecture
  • Visiting in the evening for dining and entertainment
  • Checking the events calendar for special activities
  • Wearing comfortable walking shoes
  • Bringing a camera to capture the historic details

Heritage Park Historical Village

Step back in time at Heritage Park Historical Village, Canada's largest living history museum. This 127-acre park brings Alberta's history to life with authentic buildings, costumed interpreters, and working historical artifacts.

Experience the Past

Heritage Park offers four distinct historical areas, each representing a different era in Alberta's development:

1860s Fur Trading Fort

Experience life in a Hudson's Bay Company trading post, complete with authentic buildings and demonstrations of traditional crafts.

1880s Pre-Railway Settlement

Explore a pioneer settlement with a blacksmith shop, general store, and one-room schoolhouse.

1910s Prairie Town

Visit a bustling prairie town with a working bakery, historic homes, and a vintage amusement park.

1930s-1950s Gasoline Alley

Discover Canada's largest collection of antique vehicles and gas station memorabilia.

Special Attractions

  • Working steam train rides
  • Authentic paddlewheel boat cruises on the Glenmore Reservoir
  • Historic midway with vintage rides
  • Traditional bakery with fresh goods
  • Seasonal events and festivals

Plan Your Visit

To make the most of your visit to Heritage Park, we recommend:

  • Allowing at least 4-5 hours to explore the entire park
  • Wearing comfortable walking shoes
  • Bringing sunscreen and water during summer months
  • Checking the daily schedule for special demonstrations and events
  • Considering a guided tour for a more in-depth experience

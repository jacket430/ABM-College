St. Mary's Cathedral

Experience the spiritual and architectural beauty of St. Mary's Cathedral, a stunning example of Gothic Revival architecture in the heart of Calgary. This historic landmark serves as the mother church of the Roman Catholic Diocese of Calgary and stands as a testament to the city's religious heritage.

Architectural Features

St. Mary's Cathedral showcases remarkable architectural elements:

Exterior Design

Admire the cathedral's striking Gothic Revival architecture, featuring pointed arches, flying buttresses, and a majestic bell tower that dominates the downtown skyline.

Interior Splendor

Marvel at the beautiful stained glass windows, intricate wood carvings, and the impressive pipe organ that fills the space with sacred music.

Historical Significance

Learn about the cathedral's construction in 1955 and its role as a spiritual center for Calgary's Catholic community for over half a century.

Art and Decorations

Discover the religious artwork, statues, and liturgical furnishings that enhance the spiritual atmosphere of this sacred space.

Visitor Experience

  • Self-guided tours of the cathedral
  • Attend daily Mass and special services
  • Explore the peaceful prayer garden
  • View special religious art exhibitions
  • Attend musical performances and concerts

Visitor Information

Location

219 18th Avenue SW, Calgary, AB

Located in downtown Calgary, near the Calgary Tower and easily accessible by public transit.

Hours of Operation

  • Daily: 7:00 AM - 7:00 PM
  • Mass times vary by day
  • Special services during religious holidays
  • Guided tours by appointment

Accessibility

St. Mary's Cathedral is accessible and includes:

  • Wheelchair-accessible entrance
  • Accessible washrooms
  • Assistive listening devices available
  • Accessible parking nearby
  • Service animals welcome

Plan Your Visit

To make the most of your visit to St. Mary's Cathedral, we recommend:

  • Checking the Mass schedule if you wish to attend services
  • Visiting during daylight hours to appreciate the stained glass
  • Being respectful of ongoing religious services
  • Taking time to explore the prayer garden
  • Contacting the parish office for guided tour arrangements

Glenbow Museum

Discover the rich history and culture of Western Canada at the Glenbow Museum, one of Canada's largest museums. With over a million artifacts and artworks, the museum offers fascinating insights into the region's past, present, and future.

Collections and Exhibitions

The Glenbow Museum features diverse collections and exhibitions:

Western Canadian History

Explore the stories of Indigenous peoples, early settlers, and the development of Western Canada through artifacts, photographs, and interactive displays.

Art Collection

View an impressive collection of Canadian and international art, including works by the Group of Seven, contemporary Indigenous artists, and modern masters.

Military History

Discover Canada's military heritage through uniforms, weapons, and personal stories from various conflicts and peacekeeping missions.

Mineral Collection

Marvel at one of Canada's finest mineral collections, featuring stunning specimens from around the world and Alberta's rich geological history.

Visitor Experience

  • Permanent and temporary exhibitions
  • Interactive displays and multimedia presentations
  • Guided tours and educational programs
  • Research library and archives
  • Gift shop with unique souvenirs

Visitor Information

Location

130 9th Avenue SE, Calgary, AB

Located in downtown Calgary, adjacent to the Calgary Tower and easily accessible by public transit.

Hours of Operation

  • Tuesday to Sunday: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
  • Closed Mondays and statutory holidays
  • Extended hours during special exhibitions
  • Last admission 30 minutes before closing

Accessibility

The Glenbow Museum is fully accessible and includes:

  • Wheelchair-accessible entrance and elevators
  • Accessible washrooms
  • Assistive listening devices available
  • Accessible parking nearby
  • Service animals welcome

Plan Your Visit

To make the most of your visit to the Glenbow Museum, we recommend:

  • Allowing 2-3 hours for a complete visit
  • Checking the calendar for special exhibitions and events
  • Taking advantage of guided tours for deeper insights
  • Visiting the research library for in-depth study
  • Exploring the surrounding downtown attractions

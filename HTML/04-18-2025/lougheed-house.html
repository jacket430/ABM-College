Lougheed House

Step back in time at Lougheed House, a beautifully restored 1891 sandstone mansion that offers a glimpse into Calgary's early days. This National Historic Site showcases the elegance of the Victorian era and the story of one of Calgary's founding families.

Historical Significance

Lougheed House represents an important chapter in Calgary's history:

The Mansion

Explore the 14,000 square foot sandstone mansion, featuring original woodwork, stained glass, and period furnishings that reflect the lifestyle of Calgary's early elite.

The Gardens

Stroll through the restored Victorian gardens, featuring heritage plants and design elements that recreate the original landscape of the estate.

The Lougheed Family

Learn about Senator James Lougheed and his family, who played a significant role in Calgary's development and Alberta's political history.

Restoration

Discover the meticulous restoration process that brought this historic landmark back to its original grandeur after years of neglect.

Visitor Experience

  • Guided tours of the mansion and gardens
  • Special exhibitions and events
  • Afternoon tea in the historic setting
  • Educational programs and workshops
  • Wedding and event venue rentals

Visitor Information

Location

707 13th Avenue SW, Calgary, AB

Located in the Beltline neighborhood, within walking distance of downtown Calgary.

Hours of Operation

  • Tuesday to Sunday: 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM
  • Closed Mondays and statutory holidays
  • Afternoon tea service: Wednesday to Sunday, 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM
  • Special evening events as scheduled

Accessibility

Lougheed House is committed to accessibility and includes:

  • Wheelchair-accessible entrance and washrooms
  • Elevator access to upper floors
  • Assistive listening devices available
  • Accessible parking nearby
  • Service animals welcome

Plan Your Visit

To make the most of your visit to Lougheed House, we recommend:

  • Booking a guided tour in advance
  • Reserving afternoon tea for a special experience
  • Checking the calendar for special events and exhibitions
  • Allowing 1-2 hours for a complete visit
  • Exploring the surrounding Beltline neighborhood

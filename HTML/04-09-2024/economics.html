"Economics"

Course Description

Welcome to "Economics," where we explore the fascinating world of imaginary numbers and pretend money. This course examines the theoretical foundations of economic systems that don't actually exist, from the barter of invisible goods to the trading of conceptual services. Through rigorous analysis of hypothetical scenarios, students will master the art of making up numbers and pretending they mean something.

Course Objectives

  • Master the fundamental principles of imaginary economics
  • Develop advanced techniques in pretend money management
  • Analyze the psychological impact of fictional market forces
  • Study the historical evolution of made-up economic theories
  • Write a thesis on "The Quantum Nature of Imaginary Money: A Theoretical Analysis"
